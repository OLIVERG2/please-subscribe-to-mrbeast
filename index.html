System
Overview

Region: Southeast Asia / Pacific
Status: Hyper-Biodiverse

The Coral Triangle represents the highest density of marine life on Earth. Spanning six countries, it forms the global epicenter of marine conservation efforts. This region is not just a habitat; it is an biological engine driving the health of the entire Pacific.

With over 600 species of reef-building corals alone, the structural complexity rivals any man-made architecture. It provides sustainable livelihoods for over 120 million people who rely directly on these reefs for food and coastal protection.

Species
Inventory

Fish Species: 3,000+
Coral Species: 76% Global

Inventory management in the Coral Triangle reveals staggering numbers. 37% of the world’s coral reef fish species and 56% of the ornamental coral reef fish are found here. It is the only place on Earth where six of the world's seven marine turtle species reside.

The "Amazon of the Seas" acts as a nursery for the world’s tuna supply. Without the nurseries located within the Triangle, global tuna populations—a multi-billion dollar industry—would face immediate and catastrophic collapse.

Future
Protection

Goal: 30x30 Protected
Threat Level: High

Conservation efforts are now moving toward high-tech monitoring. Using satellite data and AI, scientists are tracking coral bleaching in real-time. The goal is to establish resilient Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) that can withstand rising sea temperatures.
