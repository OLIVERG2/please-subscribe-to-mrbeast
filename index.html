THE TRIANGLE

The Global Epicenter of Marine Life

Overview

Six Nations, One Ecosystem

Spanning Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, and Timor-Leste, the Coral Triangle is the world's ultimate underwater frontier. It covers only 1.6% of the planet's oceanic area but holds 76% of all known coral species.

Biodiversity

The Amazon of the Seas

Nature here operates at a different scale. With over 6,000 species of reef fish and vital nesting grounds for six out of the world's seven marine turtle species, the complexity of this biological engine is unmatched by any other marine environment on Earth.

Conservation

A Fragile Heritage

High performance requires balance. Just as a machine needs precise tuning, the Coral Triangle requires intense protection against climate change and overfishing. This section is yours to define the future of marine conservation.
